      Boundary-Line

      Benefits and key features

      The ideal complement to the wealth of political, social and demographic information now available, Boundary-Line is an invaluable tool for a variety of applications.

      • Identify all the towns, villages and environmentally-sensitive sites for which your council is responsible.
      • Analyse social, economic and population screening to ensure that health service provision is effective and targeted.
      • Identify the council responsible for the land or building for which you intend to seek planning permission, whether you're enlarging your business premises or simply extending your house.
      • Target customers within specific areas for direct marketing and field sales purposes.

      View the capabilities of Boundary-Line online in our Election maps application.

      For forward dated boundary polygons for wards, please see Boundary-Line Support.

      An OS OpenData product

      Boundary-Line is FREE to view, download and use for commercial, educational and personal purposes.

      ONS statistical codes

      Link Boundary-Line polygons with demographic and census data for real insights into socio-economic trends.

      Current ceremonial counties

      Download for free and view the current ceremonial county boundaries of Great Britain, such as Bedfordshire, Berkshire and the West Midlands.

      Case studies

      Ordnance Survey maps out the future of the Government’s regional aid policy

      The Department for Business, Innovation and Skills (BIS) has launched a new web portal powered by Ordnance Survey map data to underpin the development of the 2014–20 UK Assisted Areas Map.

      Mapping the way to economic growth using digital data from Ordnance Survey

      In July 2013, Sir Andrew Witty, in conjunction with The Department for Business, Innovation and Skills (BIS), published the preliminary findings of his independent review of universities and growth, underpinned by digital mapping data from Ordnance Survey.

      Ordnance Survey mapping data used to further enhance consultation portal

      The Local Government Boundary Commission for England (LGBCE) has introduced new functionality to its consultation portal, which uses detailed mapping from Ordnance Survey to get closer to customers and provide new, more detailed intelligence to support boundary reviews.

      Making complex statistical information accessible and easy-to-understand

      Ordnance Survey mapping data, supplied under the Public Sector Mapping Agreement (PSMA), provides a consistent geographic framework for collating, presenting and analysing complex data for the Office for National Statistics (ONS).

      Improving primary care out-of-hours services in Somerset

      During 2012–13, Somerset Clinical Commissioning Group (CCG) undertook a strategic review of the primary care out-of-hours service across the county. South West Commissioning Support supported the CCG to deliver the review programme.

      FormatESRI Shapefile and MapInfo TAB
      Data structureVector
      AttributesBoundary-Line contains every type of administrative area for England, Scotland and Wales.
      ScaleAll boundaries are captured and maintained against a generalised 1:10 000 Scale Raster product.
